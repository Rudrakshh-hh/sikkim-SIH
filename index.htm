Other Monasteries of Sikkim

Tashiding Monastery

A sacred monastery in West Sikkim, known for the annual Bhumchu Festival.

Built in 1717, Tashiding is one of the holiest monasteries in Sikkim. The monastery hosts the famous Bhumchu festival, where a sacred vase of water is unveiled to predict the future of Sikkim.

Phodong Monastery

A 18th-century monastery famous for its vibrant murals and festivals.

Located in North Sikkim, Phodong Monastery was built in the early 18th century. It belongs to the Kagyu sect of Buddhism and is renowned for its exquisite murals and cham dance festivals.

Ralong Monastery

A major monastery of the Kagyu sect, known for the grand Pang Lhabsol festival.

Ralong Monastery, located near Ravangla, was built after the 12th Karmapa visited Tibet. It is famous for hosting the colorful Pang Lhabsol festival and houses an impressive collection of thangkas.

Rumtek Monastery

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, is one of the most significant monasteries in Sikkim. Originally built in the 16th century, it was rebuilt by the 16th Karmapa in the 1960s. The monastery houses sacred relics, a golden stupa, and serves as the seat of the Kagyu sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

Pemayangtse Monastery

Pemayangtse Monastery, built in the 17th century, is one of the oldest and most prestigious monasteries in Sikkim. Enchey Monastery

Enchey Monastery, located near Gangtok, was built in the 19th century by Lama Druptob Karpo. It belongs to the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism and is surrounded by scenic beauty. The monastery is associated with legends of flying monks and is especially vibrant during the Cham dance festival, held annually.
